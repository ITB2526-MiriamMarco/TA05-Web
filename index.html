Cybersecurity: Global Defense

> Status: Monitoring Traffic...

Cybersecurity is the practice of defending computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks. In a hyper-connected world, the complexity of these threats grows exponentially.

1. Core Pillars (CIA Triad):

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring that data is accessible only to those authorized to have access.
  • Integrity: Safeguarding the accuracy and completeness of information and processing methods.
  • Availability: Ensuring that authorized users have access to information and associated assets when required.

2. Key Defense Areas:

To build a resilient system, we must cover multiple layers of security:

  • Network Security: Securing a computer network from intruders, whether targeted attackers or opportunistic malware.
  • Application Security: Keeping software and devices free of threats by implementing secure coding practices.
  • Information Security: Protecting the integrity and privacy of data, both in storage and in transit.
  • Operational Security: Processes for handling and protecting data assets (permissions and workflows).

3. Threat Landscape:

Modern attacks use sophisticated methods to bypass standard firewalls:

  • Zero-Day Exploits: Attacks targeting undisclosed vulnerabilities.
  • Social Engineering: Manipulating users into divesting confidential info.
  • Ransomware: Encrypting files and demanding payment for the key.
